## üíº SECTION 1: Email Writing Prompts

### 1.1 Cold Outreach Email
```
Act as a professional copywriter. Write a cold outreach email that:
- Opens with a personalized hook relevant to [RECIPIENT'S INDUSTRY]
- Clearly states the value proposition in 2-3 sentences
- Includes one specific, relevant case study or statistic
- Ends with a clear, low-commitment call to action
- Keeps the total length under 150 words

Topic/Niche: [YOUR PRODUCT OR SERVICE]
Target Audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER]
Key Benefit: [MAIN VALUE YOU PROVIDE]
```

### 1.2 Follow-Up Email
```
Write a professional follow-up email for someone who:
- Received my initial email about [TOPIC] [NUMBER] days ago
- Hasn't responded yet
- Is a [DECISION MAKER/INFLUENCER/OTHER] at [COMPANY TYPE]
- Our solution specifically helps with [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT]

The email should:
- Acknowledge their busy schedule
- Add one new piece of value or insight
- Keep it short (under 100 words)
- End with a specific meeting time offer
```

### 1.3 Introduction Email (New Connection)
```
Act as a networking expert. Write an introduction email that:
- Introduces me as [YOUR TITLE/ROLE] from [COMPANY]
- Explains why I'm reaching out specifically to them
- Offers genuine value (not just self-promotion)
- Suggests a brief 15-minute call
- Has a warm, professional tone

My Background: [2-3 SENTENCES ABOUT YOU]
Why Them: [SPECIFIC REASON FOR REACHING OUT]
Value I Can Offer: [WHAT YOU BRING TO THE TABLE]
```

### 1.4 Apology Email (Customer Service)
```
Write a professional customer service apology email that:
- Acknowledges the customer's issue with [SPECIFIC PROBLEM]
- Takes full responsibility without excuses
- Explains what went wrong briefly and honestly
- Offers a specific solution or compensation
- Ends with appreciation and invitation for feedback

Customer Issue: [DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM]
Solution Offered: [HOW YOU'LL FIX IT]
Compensation (if any): [WHAT THEY GET]
```

### 1.5 Interview Follow-Up Email
```
Write a post-interview thank you email that:
- References specific topics discussed in the interview
- Reiterates enthusiasm for the [ROLE] position
- Adds one new insight or value I can bring
- References connection made with [INTERVIEWER'S NAME]
- Keeps it concise and memorable

Position: [JOB TITLE]
Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Key Discussion Points: [TOPICS TALKED ABOUT]
Interviewer: [NAME]
Additional Insight: [NEW VALUE TO ADD]
```

## üìù SECTION 2: Blog & Article Prompts

### 2.1 How-To Blog Post
```
Act as an expert content writer. Write a comprehensive how-to blog post about:

Title: "How to [ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME] in [TIMEFRAME/BETTER WAY]"

Structure:
1. Compelling hook (problem-solution format)
2. Brief explanation of why this matters
3. Step-by-step guide with clear headings
4. Pro tips for best results
5. Common mistakes to avoid
6. Conclusion with call to action

Target Audience: [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]
Desired Outcome: [WHAT READERS WILL ACHIEVE]
Tone: [PROFESSIONAL/CASUAL/FRIENDLY]
Word Count: [TARGET WORD COUNT]
```

### 2.2 Listicle Article
```
Act as a viral content creator. Write a listicle article:

Title: "[NUMBER] [ADJECTIVE] Ways to [BENEFIT FOR READER]"

Include:
- Attention-grabbing introduction
- [NUMBER] actionable items
- Each item should have 2-3 sentences of explanation
- Real-world examples where possible
- Inspiring conclusion

Topic: [SUBJECT MATTER]
Number of Items: [HOW MANY TIPS/STRATEGIES]
Reader Level: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED]
Unique Angle: [WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT]
```

### 2.3 Comparison Article
```
Act as an industry analyst. Write a comprehensive comparison article:

Title: "[OPTION A] vs [OPTION B]: Which Is Better for [TARGET AUDIENCE]?"

Include:
- Brief introduction to both options
- Side-by-side comparison of key features
- Pros and cons of each
- Best use cases for each
- Final recommendation based on specific scenarios

Option A: [FIRST PRODUCT/METHOD/OPTION]
Option B: [SECOND PRODUCT/METHOD/OPTION]
Target Audience: [WHO IS THIS FOR]
Primary Decision Factor: [WHAT MATTERS MOST]
```

### 2.4 Thought Leadership Article
```
Act as a thought leader in [YOUR INDUSTRY]. Write an opinion piece that:

Title: "The Truth About [COMMON MYTH] in [INDUSTRY]"

Structure:
- Start with a bold, contrarian statement
- Back up with data and real-world examples
- Challenge conventional wisdom
- Provide evidence for alternative view
- End with actionable takeaways

Common Myth: [WIDELY BELIEVED MYTH]
Your Position: [YOUR CONTRARIAN VIEW]
Evidence: [DATA OR EXAMPLES SUPPORTING YOUR VIEW]
Call to Action: [WHAT READERS SHOULD DO]
```

### 2.5 Case Study Article
```
Act as a content strategist. Write a case study article:

Title: "How [COMPANY NAME] Increased [METRIC] by [PERCENTAGE] Using [YOUR METHOD]"

Structure:
- Company background (2-3 sentences)
- The challenge they faced
- The solution implemented
- Results (with specific numbers)
- Key takeaways for readers

Company: [CLIENT COMPANY]
Industry: [WHAT THEY DO]
Challenge: [PROBLEM THEY HAD]
Solution: [WHAT YOU DID]
Results: [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES]
```
